# How-Do-Concrete-Vaults-Actually-Work-

## User Perspective

Let’s be honest.

You deposit into one of the **Concrete vaults**… and your tokens disappear.

In their place, you get **vault shares**.

Then you check the dashboard and see things like:
- **eRate**
- **NAV**
- a balance that’s moving — but not in the way you expected

And the first thought is usually:

> “Okay… what’s actually going on here?”

That confusion is completely normal.

Because under the surface, **DeFi vaults** work very differently from a simple wallet or staking app.

So let’s walk through it — clearly, and without overcomplicating it.

---

## Vault Shares & eRate (Where It Clicks)

Here’s the key idea:

> When you deposit, you’re not just adding funds — you’re buying ownership.

Think of the vault like a growing pool of capital.

- The pool = the vault  
- Your **vault shares** = your ownership in that pool  

Now here’s the part that trips people up:

Your number of shares doesn’t increase over time.

Instead…

> Each share becomes more valuable.

That’s what **eRate** represents.

**eRate is the value of a single share.**

- At the start → 1 share might equal $1  
- As the vault performs → 1 share might equal $1.12, $1.25, or higher  

So your position grows not because you have more shares —  
but because each share is worth more.

That’s the core mechanic behind **automated compounding** in **managed DeFi**.

---

## NAV (The Big Picture Number)

Now let’s simplify **NAV**.

> NAV is just the total value of everything inside the vault.

That includes:
- all user deposits  
- all generated yield  
- all active positions  

If the vault holds $20 million total, then its NAV is $20 million.

Here’s how it connects:

- NAV = the total pool  
- Shares = your portion of that pool  

When NAV increases, the value of each share increases.

Which means:

> Your ownership stays the same — but what you own becomes more valuable.

## Why Time Is the Real Advantage

This is the part most people underestimate.

**Concrete vaults aren’t built for quick flips.**

They’re built for time.

Because in reality:
- strategies need time to play out  
- capital needs to be deployed and repositioned  
- fees and execution costs need to be absorbed  
- compounding needs time to stack  

A helpful way to think about it:

> This isn’t a trade — it’s a process.

Early on, growth might feel slow or even uneven.

But over time, something important happens:

> The system starts building on itself.

Returns generate more returns.  
Decisions compound.  
Efficiency improves.

And one truth stands out:

> Most people don’t lose in DeFi because systems fail —  
> they lose because they don’t stay long enough.

## Active Management (What’s Happening Behind the Scenes)

It’s easy to assume a vault just holds funds.

But **Concrete vaults** don’t sit idle.

They operate as a layer of **managed DeFi**, where capital is continuously deployed onchain and adjusted over time.

Behind the scenes, the system is:

- allocating capital across different strategies  
- rebalancing as market conditions shift  
- optimizing positions for better outcomes  
- managing risk while targeting yield  

A simple way to picture it:

> Your capital is being actively put to work — not stored.

This is what makes modern **DeFi vaults** powerful.

You’re not just accessing yield —  
you’re accessing a system that *manages how that yield is created*.

## How It All Comes Together

From the outside, it looks simple.

But here’s what’s actually happening step by step:

1. You deposit into the vault  
2. You receive **vault shares** representing your ownership  
3. Capital is deployed through onchain strategies  
4. Yield is generated and added back into the vault  
5. The vault’s **NAV** increases  
6. The **eRate** rises  
7. Your shares become more valuable over time  

No manual rebalancing.  
No strategy switching.  
No constant monitoring.

Just participation in a system designed to optimize itself.
